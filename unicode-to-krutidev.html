Why is Unicode to Kruti Dev Conversion Necessary?

In the digital landscape of India, Hindi typing is divided into two primary technologies: Unicode and Legacy Fonts (Non-Unicode).

Understanding Unicode (Mangal)

Unicode is the international standard. When you type on WhatsApp, Facebook, or Google using your phone, you are using Unicode (usually Mangal or Nirmala UI font). The computer understands the character itself, not just the shape.

Understanding Kruti Dev 010

Kruti Dev is a "Legacy Font" based on the Remington typewriter layout. It doesn't actually store Hindi characters; instead, it maps Hindi shapes to English keys. For example, typing 'D' in Kruti Dev produces 'क'. Because of this mapping, standard Unicode text looks like gibberish if you just change the font to Kruti Dev. You need a Unicode to Kruti Dev 10 Generator to mathematically re-map these characters.

Use Cases

  • Typing Exams: Exams like CPCT often have sections requiring proficiency in specific layouts.
