Understanding GST: Inclusive vs. Exclusive Calculations

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a value-added tax levied on most goods and services sold for domestic consumption. Whether you are a business owner issuing invoices or a consumer trying to understand your bill, knowing how to calculate GST is essential. Our online GST Calculator simplifies this process.

What is GST Exclusive Amount?

"GST Exclusive" refers to the value of goods or services before the tax is applied. This is the base price. When you create an invoice, you typically take this base price and add the GST percentage to it to find the final payable amount.

Formula: Total = Net Price + (Net Price Ã— GST%)

What is GST Inclusive Amount?

"GST Inclusive" refers to the total value of the product after the tax has been included. This is often the MRP (Maximum Retail Price) you see in stores. Sometimes, you need to work backward to find out how much of that price was the actual product cost and how much was tax. This is called removing or reverse-calculating GST.

Formula: GST Amount = Total - [Total / (1 + GST%)]

Why Use an Online GST Calculator?

While the math might seem simple for round numbers (like 10% of 100), it becomes complex with real-world figures and rates like 18% or 12.5%. A slight error in tax calculation can lead to billing discrepancies and compliance issues. Our tool ensures you get the exact figures down to the decimal, helping in accurate accounting and financial planning.

GST Rate Tiers

Different countries and regions have different GST slabs. For example, in India, goods are categorized under 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28% slabs. In Australia, the standard rate is 10%. Our tool allows you to input any custom rate, making it suitable for users worldwide.
